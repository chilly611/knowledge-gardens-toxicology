02Erin Brockovich / Hinkley chromium-6 case

Case details: Anderson v. Pacific Gas & Electric Co. (Hinkley groundwater contamination, Kern County, California). Settled 1996 for $333 million — the largest environmental class-action settlement in U.S. history at that time[6].

Dr. Dahlgren’s documented role

In a 2022 feature in the San Francisco Bay View, Dr. Dahlgren is quoted describing his expert toxicology work in the Hinkley case[7]:

“Chromium was the poison in the Brockovich case… Chromium is super toxic and super carcinogenic.”
— Dr. James G. Dahlgren, San Francisco Bay View, June 2022 [7]

He noted in comparative context that toxins at other contaminated sites (Hunters Point Naval Shipyard) are “even more deadly” and that the Hinkley case involved “about 150 plaintiffs who got money”[7].

The 2000 Steven Soderbergh film Erin Brockovich (Universal Pictures; Julia Roberts won Best Actress) dramatised the case from the legal-clerk perspective. Dr. Dahlgren’s role was on the medical/toxicological expert-witness side, not the legal advocacy side — that was Erin Brockovich, the legal clerk. The two are distinct people with distinct roles: Brockovich was the legal facilitator; Dahlgren was the independent medical expert assessing health effects[6][7].

Public quote on Hinkley health effects

“These all could be an aberration, but you have a whole cluster of rare and unusual cancers.”
— Dr. Dahlgren, comments during a health-effects presentation [7]
Honest caveat

No direct quote from Erin Brockovich (the person) publicly crediting Dr. Dahlgren by name was found in web search. His role is documented in expert-witness databases and contemporaneous news coverage but is not prominently featured in Brockovich’s own published interviews or memoir. For marketing purposes, position him as “the M.D. toxicologist behind the Hinkley case” with attribution to expert-witness directories and the 2022 Bay View interview — not as someone Brockovich named publicly.

03Other documented cases

Case: Erickson v. Monsanto / Pharmacia (Sky Valley PCB) Jurisdiction: King County Superior Court, WA Years: Trial 2021; appeals 2024–25

Sky Valley Education Center PCB litigation

Testifying expert witness on toxicology and health causation[8]. Initial jury verdict $185 million (compensatory + punitive damages); upheld by Washington Supreme Court (6-3) October 2025[9][10]. December 10, 2025: Monsanto settled the Erickson case plus 200+ additional plaintiffs on confidential terms[11]. Dr. Dahlgren testified that PCBs from caulk and fluorescent lighting fixtures in aging school buildings caused neurological and hormonal injury to three lead plaintiffs (Kerry Erickson, Michelle Leahy, Joyce Marquardt) and other exposed teachers[8][11].

Case: Crane Co. v. DeLisle Jurisdiction: Florida Fourth District / FL Supreme Court Years: Trial 2016; appellate Sept 2016; FL Supreme Court Oct 2018

Asbestos litigation — Daubert reversal

Expert witness for plaintiff (DeLisle) on mesothelioma causation. $8 million verdict for plaintiff; later reversed on Daubert grounds, then reinstated on appeal[12][13]. Key testimony: Dr. Dahlgren opined that “every exposure” to asbestos above background level constitutes a substantial contributing cause of mesothelioma. The Fourth District Court of Appeal initially ruled his opinion inadmissible under Daubert (insufficient methodology/data, particularly for chrysotile asbestos). The Florida Supreme Court later reversed this, ruling the Daubert amendment unconstitutional and restoring his testimony. This is the strongest single argument that his testimony survives Daubert challenges.

Location: Wood treatment plant, Mississippi Population: 1,269 exposed; 214 in detail Publication: Environmental Health Perspectives, 2003

Wood treatment plant exposure (creosote & pentachlorophenol)

  • Exposed residents: 17.8% diagnosed bronchitis vs. 5.8% controls[14]
  • Exposed residents: 40.5% asthma history vs. 11% controls[14]
  • Attic dust contaminated with pentachlorophenol (PCP, carcinogenic)[14]
  • PAH-DNA adducts 4.7× higher in exposed group, indicating active exposure to polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons[14]

Peer-reviewed publication in Environmental Health Perspectives (2003) and Archives of Environmental & Occupational Health (2007)[14].

Case: DuPont C8/PFOA Class Action Locations: Wood County WI; Parkersburg WV

PFOA / C8 contamination

Retained by plaintiffs in class action against DuPont. Key findings: overall cancer prevalence significantly higher in PFOA-exposed population vs. general population; elevated rates in ages 24–65; increased prevalence of atypical cancers (Hodgkin’s lymphoma, leukemia, multiple myeloma)[15]. DuPont contested findings, arguing PFOA is not a human carcinogen and Dr. Dahlgren’s methodology was flawed; case is referenced in epidemiological literature on PFOA toxicity[15].

Population: ~40,000 9/11 rescue/recovery workers Publication: Chemosphere, 2007

World Trade Center exposure (persistent organic pollutants)

Body burden study of PCBs, polychlorinated dibenzofurans (PCDFs), and dioxins in 9/11 rescue and recovery workers exposed to ultra-fine particulate dust. Published in Chemosphere 69(8) with co-authors Marie Cecchini, Harpreet Takhar, Olaf Paepke (UCLA Occupational Medicine program)[16]. The study received critical commentary from other scientists regarding methodological concerns — a fact worth noting for completeness.

07Assessment of the Hinkley/Brockovich connection

What is verified
  • Dr. Dahlgren was a toxicology expert in Anderson v. Pacific Gas & Electric (1996 settlement, $333 million)[6][7]
  • He provided expert opinion on health effects of chromium exposure[7]
  • The case became the basis for the 2000 film Erin Brockovich[6]
  • A direct public quote attributed to Dr. Dahlgren on chromium toxicity exists[7]
What is NOT verified
  • Direct quote or statement from Erin Brockovich (the legal clerk) publicly crediting Dr. Dahlgren by name
  • Specific testimony transcripts or deposition excerpts from the Hinkley trial (the case settled before trial completion)
  • Detailed description of his sampling methodology or precise expert role (consultant vs. testifying expert vs. health-effects assessor)
  • Any interview between Dr. Dahlgren and Brockovich or her team in the public record

Honest assessment

Dr. Dahlgren’s involvement in Hinkley is documented in expert-witness networks and news coverage, but he is not prominently featured in popular accounts of the case. The 2000 film focuses on Erin Brockovich (the clerk) and her legal team; Dr. Dahlgren’s behind-the-scenes toxicology role is real but not a starring element of the Brockovich narrative.

For lawyer-facing marketing: Position him as “the M.D. toxicologist behind the Hinkley case” with full attribution and context; cite the 2022 Bay View interview and expert-witness directories; do not overstate Brockovich’s public acknowledgment because it did not happen. The single strongest narrative anchor for survivability under Daubert is not Hinkley but Crane Co. v. DeLisle, where the Florida Supreme Court reversed a lower court’s Daubert exclusion of his testimony.

Why this exists

Toxic-tort prep is paralegal hours, ChatGPT hallucinations, and the constant risk that opposing counsel impeaches your expert at the Daubert stage.

Every plaintiffs’ firm working a chemical-injury case rebuilds the same dossier from scratch. Forty hours of paralegal time per substance, IARC monographs and ATSDR profiles re-typed into Word, verbatim quotes manually transcribed and re-checked, expert disclosures drafted under FRCP 26(a)(2) with citation lists that opposing counsel will probe for the smallest gap.

Asking ChatGPT speeds the front end up but introduces a new exposure: roughly one in four AI citations is fabricated, paraphrased without attribution, or attached to a study that says something different from the conclusion the model summarised. That risk shows up at the Daubert stage. It does not survive cross.

The Toxicology Knowledge Garden is the alternative. A curated, audit-trailed evidence graph for chemical and biological hazards, anchored on a sitting M.D. toxicologist with five decades of expert-witness work. Every claim shows its sources, its tier, its retrieval date, and its curator. Every export is a chambers-ready PDF with page numbers, citations footer, and a signature line.

The 2000 Steven Soderbergh film starring Julia Roberts dramatised the Hinkley case from the legal-clerk vantage point. Behind the legal advocacy was the medical/toxicological assessment of chromium-6 health effects in the Hinkley plaintiffs — expert work attributed to Dr. Dahlgren in expert-witness directories and contemporaneous reporting. The case settled in 1996 for $333 million, then the largest environmental class-action settlement in U.S. history.

Roughly two decades later, in Crane Co. v. DeLisle (Florida), the Fourth District Court of Appeal initially excluded Dr. Dahlgren’s expert testimony on asbestos causation under Daubert. The Florida Supreme Court reversed, holding the Daubert amendment unconstitutional and reinstating his testimony along with the $8 million jury verdict for the plaintiff. Forty-plus years of cross-examination and the testimony stands.

Daubert prep

Daubert Pre-Motion Packet

Substance-specific exhibit packet: certified-vs-contested ratio, regulatory tier-1 source list, peer-review tier-3 cohort summaries, contradicting-source side-by-side. Designed to anchor or defend a Daubert challenge.

Causation

Causation Brief

Substance × endpoint dossier with confidence score, mechanism of action, dose-response curve, biomarker panel, and full evidence trail. For motions in limine and direct testimony prep.

FRCP 26(a)(2)

Expert Witness Pre-Disclosure

Dr. Dahlgren’s curated case file on a specific substance, formatted for FRCP 26(a)(2)(B) compliance. Credentials, prior testimony list, publications, opinion summary, basis-and-reasons section, fee schedule.

Cross-Examination

Opposing-Expert Vulnerability Brief

Surfaces every contested claim where opposing counsel’s expert may rely on a single regulatory body in disagreement with the wider literature (e.g., EPA non-finding vs. IARC 2A on glyphosate). Cross-examination ammunition.

Quote Bank

Daubert-Ready Claim Set

Verbatim-quote-grade claim collection. Each claim with supporting and contradicting verbatim quotes from primary sources, page citations, retrieval timestamps. Filable as exhibits without re-typing.

Discovery

Case Timeline + Document Register

Pre-formatted matter chronology and document index for any toxic-tort case in the platform’s graph. Saves the discovery production assembly phase. Sky Valley case ships with 13 events and 31 documents already cited.

Mass Tort

Comparable-Case Lookup

List of prior cases by substance, jurisdiction, verdict, and settlement size. For early-stage viability assessment of a contemplated litigation track. Useful for partner-meeting decision packets.

Strategy

Settlement-Posture Brief

Quantified case-strength score combining certified-claim ratio, regulatory-vs-peer-review balance, and historical comparable verdicts. Supports settlement-vs-trial decision and demand-letter authoring.
